# The Changing Nature of Entrepreneurship in the United States

Evidence from Shopify

[Lisa Abraham](/about/people/a/abraham_lisa.html), [Gabriel W. Hassler](/about/people/h/hassler_gabriel_w.html), [Benjamin K. Master](/about/people/m/master_benjamin_k.html), [Brian Phillips](/about/people/p/phillips_brian.html)

Published Jul 31, 2024

![Cover: The Changing Nature of Entrepreneurship in the United States](/content/rand/pubs/working_papers/WRA3370-1/_jcr_content/par/product.fit.264x264.jpeg/1722370296631.jpeg)

This paper uses data from Shopify to provide insight into the growing space of entrepreneurs using online platforms in the United States. Both Black and female entrepreneurs are overrepresented on the Shopify platform, and a sizable share of businesses experience initial growth. Minority owners report valuing supports associated with the platform more than nonminority owners, but also face more challenges, particularly around financing.
