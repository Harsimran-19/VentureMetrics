# Startup Ecosystem in Hong Kong: Meaning, Diagram & Guide - TEC Hong Kong

Government policy continues to play a pivotal role in strengthening Hong Kong’s ecosystem for startups, contributing to its growing maturity. Initiatives such as the Innovation and Technology Fund (ITF)"), the Startup Financing Scheme, and increased investment in R&D infrastructure have created a more supportive environment for early-stage ventures. The launch of the Innovation and Technology Development Blueprint also provides a long-term strategic vision to guide ecosystem growth. As these policies take effect, Hong Kong is shifting from a primarily finance-driven economy to one that actively nurtures innovation and entrepreneurship. This maturing landscape reflects not only improved access to resources but also stronger collaboration among government bodies, academia, corporations, and [...] These entrepreneurs are supported by a variety of funding sources, including angel investors and venture capital firms that supply critical early-stage and growth capital. Incubators and accelerators further enhance this support with seed funding, structured growth programs, expert mentorship, and access to robust professional networks.

Government involvement is equally vital. Through forward-thinking policy, investment in infrastructure such as science parks and digital connectivity, and initiatives like grants, tax breaks, and co-investment schemes, governments help reduce risk and foster innovation at scale.
