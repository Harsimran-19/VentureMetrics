Early-stage startups building initial products need seed capital and infrastructure. University spinouts from the six designated institutions should start with TSSSU, potentially progressing to RAISe+ for exceptionally ambitious deep-tech ventures. Digital technology entrepreneurs find comprehensive ecosystem support through Cyberport’s incubation programme. Hardware, robotics, and biotech ventures requiring specialised labs belong at HKSTP – Incu-Tech for general deep tech, Incu-Bio for life sciences. Young founders aged 18-35 can access interest-free loans through Youth Business Hong Kong. [...] ### Hong Kong’s Policy Drivers Behind the Money

Hong Kong’s funding architecture reflects specific economic priorities outlined in theHong Kong Innovation and Technology Development Blueprint. Four objectives dominate: promoting “new industrialisation” through R&D commercialisation, empowering SMEs (they constituteover 98% of local businesses), strengthening Hong Kong’s super-connector role for Greater Bay Area integration, and accelerating the green transition.

Funding has evolved from broad support towards building integrated ecosystems. Newer initiatives like RAISe+ bundle funding, talent access, and commercialisation assistance.
