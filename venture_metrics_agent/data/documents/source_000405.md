# State of Venture 2025

Funding reached its highest level since 2022, with AI nearing 50% of the total. Mega-rounds drove growth. Robotics funding hit record levels. Here's what happened in venture in 2025.

## **Top stories in 2025**

### **1. Venture funding rebounds, but deals continue to drop**

After two years of decline, venture funding rebounded to $469B in 2025, up 47% YoY, with last quarter alone seeing $152B — the strongest since Q1’22.

But deal activity continued to slide. In 2025, deal count fell 17% to 29,501, while mega-rounds surged 77% to 738, capturing $307B (65% of total venture funding). Investors are increasingly writing bigger checks to fewer companies, with the biggest AI startups capturing most of the money.

[![](https://research-assets.cbinsights.com/2026/01/08122544/SoV2025_TotalFunding_1-958x1024.png)](https://research-assets.cbinsights.com/2026/01/08122544/SoV2025_TotalFunding_1.png)

![](https://research-assets.cbinsights.com/2026/01/08122544/SoV2025_TotalFunding_1-958x1024.png)

Nearly all of that capital is flowing to one place. US startups raised $328B in 2025 — 70% of global funding — approaching its 2021 record of $358B. 

Meanwhile, Asia and Europe grew modestly but remain far below their 2021 peaks. Asia increased 7% to $53B, while Europe rose from 18% to nearly $68B.

[![](https://research-assets.cbinsights.com/2026/01/08122610/SoV2025_USFunding_2-958x1024.png)](https://research-assets.cbinsights.com/2026/01/08122610/SoV2025_USFunding_2.png)

![](https://research-assets.cbinsights.com/2026/01/08122610/SoV2025_USFunding_2-958x1024.png)

Regulatory crackdowns in China are driving much of the funding decline in Asia, alongside an increased focus on corporate innovation across the continent. Europe has fewer strong AI startups than the US, so it benefited less from the AI boom.

US funding dominance means domestic shifts could ripple globally — through valuation changes that reset pricing expectations, regulations that set international precedents, or M&A trends that shape exit paths for startups worldwide.

### **2. AI accounts for nearly half of all venture funding**

AI companies raised $226B in 2025, accounting for 48% of total venture funding — the largest share on record. Investors are betting on every layer of the AI stack under the assumption of winner-take-all dynamics.

The six largest funding rounds of 2025 all went to AI companies: [OpenAI](https://www.cbinsights.com/company/openai) ($41B), [Anthropic](https://www.cbinsights.com/company/anthropic) ($32.5B), [Scale](https://www.cbinsights.com/company/scale-ai) ($14.8B), [xAI](https://www.cbinsights.com/company/xai) ($12.8B), [Databricks](https://www.cbinsights.com/company/databricks) ($5B), and [Aligned](https://www.cbinsights.com/company/aligned-energy) ($5B).

The first five focus on [foundation models](https://app.cbinsights.com/market-report/1710?tab=overview) and [application development](https://app.cbinsights.com/market-report/2312?tab=overview), while Aligned builds [AI data centers](https://app.cbinsights.com/research/report/data-center-value-chain-market-map/). Together, these companies raised $111B — 49% of all AI funding and 24% of total venture capital.

[![](https://research-assets.cbinsights.com/2026/01/08122326/SoV2025_AIpercentage_3-958x1024.png)](https://research-assets.cbinsights.com/2026/01/08122326/SoV2025_AIpercentage_3.png)

![](https://research-assets.cbinsights.com/2026/01/08122326/SoV2025_AIpercentage_3-958x1024.png)

These funding levels reflect expectations that a small number of AI companies will control their markets, as [Google](https://www.cbinsights.com/company/google) dominates search or [Microsoft](https://www.cbinsights.com/company/microsoft-bing) leads in enterprise software. Venture capital is essentially making a single bet — that AI will deliver massive returns fast enough to justify current funding totals.

If the bets pay off, these companies will define the next decade. But technical limitations for foundation models, persistently high computing costs, or regulatory restrictions could severely disrupt the entire venture market. Few other sectors can offset the damage, since venture growth now depends almost entirely on a handful of AI startups.

### **3. Robotics gains momentum going into 2026**

Robotics companies raised a record [$40.7B in 2025](https://app.cbinsights.com/top-search?tab=company&isid=0-26fc0970-29c6-544f-b487-b22036341520), accounting for 9% of all venture funding, making it one of the leading sectors alongside AI software. The sector spans [humanoid robots](https://app.cbinsights.com/research/humanoid-robots-market-map/), [autonomous vehicles](https://app.cbinsights.com/research/the-autonomous-driving-market-map/), [drones](https://app.cbinsights.com/research/drone-tech-market-map/), [robot foundation models](https://app.cbinsights.com/market-report/2745?tab=overview), and supporting [hardware](https://app.cbinsights.com/market-report/2658?tab=companies).

Physical AI — AI systems that operate in the [physical world](https://app.cbinsights.com/research/humanoid-robots-launch-ai-into-physical-world/) and learn from data rather than pre-programmed rules — is driving this growth. 

[![](https://research-assets.cbinsights.com/2026/01/08122442/SoV2025_RoboticsFunding_4-958x1024.png)](https://research-assets.cbinsights.com/2026/01/08122442/SoV2025_RoboticsFunding_4.png)

![](https://research-assets.cbinsights.com/2026/01/08122442/SoV2025_RoboticsFunding_4-958x1024.png)

[Industrial humanoid robots](https://app.cbinsights.com/market-report/2365) led all markets with [80 deals in 2025](https://app.cbinsights.com/markets?tab=index), but valuations suggest bubble risk. For example, [Figure](https://www.cbinsights.com/company/figure) reached a $39B valuation — a 15x jump in one year — with zero revenue and a 2/5 Commercial Maturity score, showing how investors are making huge bets on high-profile physical AI startups, even without proof of commercial success.

Physical AI model developers are showing the highest momentum among 1,600+ markets, with 4 of the top 10 based on [average Mosaic scores](https://app.cbinsights.com/markets?tab=index).

[![](https://research-assets.cbinsights.com/2026/01/08122415/SoV2025_PhysicalAITopMarkets_5-958x1024.png)](https://research-assets.cbinsights.com/2026/01/08122415/SoV2025_PhysicalAITopMarkets_5.png)

![](https://research-assets.cbinsights.com/2026/01/08122415/SoV2025_PhysicalAITopMarkets_5-958x1024.png)

Better robot foundation models are critical for real-world functionality. We may look back at 2025 as a turning point for physical AI, but the immediate focus is on proving out specific use cases — getting individual robots to handle narrow tasks reliably in controlled environments.

The bigger opportunity lies in coordinating fleets of robots across [factories](https://app.cbinsights.com/research/future-of-the-factory-manufacturing/), [construction sites](https://app.cbinsights.com/research/280-ai-companies-automating-the-construction-industry/), [battlefields](https://app.cbinsights.com/research/the-top-100-european-ai-aerospace-defense-startups/), and [supply chains](https://app.cbinsights.com/research/automated-warehouse-market-map/). But full “[orchestration AI](https://app.cbinsights.com/market-report/2951?tab=overview)” that manages these coordinated systems remains years away from commercialization.

### **4. Valuations skyrocket among top private companies**

The top private companies reached unprecedented valuation levels across multiple sectors in 2025.

The top 10 companies are worth over $2T combined. While AI companies captured much of the attention — OpenAI raised at a $500B valuation and Anthropic at $350B — the list reveals a concentration of capital across different industries.

[ByteDance](https://www.cbinsights.com/company/bytedance) and [SpaceX](https://www.cbinsights.com/company/space-exploration-technologies) reached valuations of $480B and $400B, respectively, demonstrating that companies with proven business models can command valuations similar to those of AI leaders. Databricks reached $134B, [Stripe](https://www.cbinsights.com/company/stripe) hit $107B, and [Canva](https://www.cbinsights.com/company/canva) climbed to $42B, each showing strong annual growth.

[![](https://research-assets.cbinsights.com/2026/01/08123055/SoV2025_Top10Valuations_6-887x1024.png)](https://research-assets.cbinsights.com/2026/01/08123055/SoV2025_Top10Valuations_6.png)

![](https://research-assets.cbinsights.com/2026/01/08123055/SoV2025_Top10Valuations_6-887x1024.png)

Valuation increases vary widely. Anthropic jumped 19x in one year as the only newcomer to the top tier. OpenAI increased 218%, while Databricks grew 116%. In contrast, non-AI companies saw more moderate gains — SpaceX increased 14%, Canva grew 32%, and [SHEIN](https://www.cbinsights.com/company/shein) remained flat at $66B.

The question is whether these premiums reflect fundamental value or a pricing disconnect that will correct.

### **5. Leading investors reinforce AI concentration**

Among CB Insights [Smart Money VCs](https://app.cbinsights.com/research/smart-money-2025/) — the 25 best-performing VC investors over the past decade — deal activity remained high in 2025.

[General Catalyst](https://www.cbinsights.com/investor/general-catalyst-partners) led with 213 deals, followed by [Andreessen Horowitz](https://www.cbinsights.com/investor/andreessen-horowitz) with 178 and [Sequoia](https://www.cbinsights.com/investor/sequoia-capital) with 139.

But volume alone is not the signal. The top 10 markets for SMVCs were all focused on AI. [Coding AI agents & copilots](https://app.cbinsights.com/market-report/2556?tab=overview) captured 22 deals, [legal AI agents & copilots](https://app.cbinsights.com/market-report/2557?tab=overview) took 20, and [end-to-end software development agents](https://app.cbinsights.com/market-report/2839?tab=overview) grabbed 17. [Multimodal AI developers](https://app.cbinsights.com/market-report/1718?tab=overview), [voice AI platforms](https://app.cbinsights.com/market-report/2733?tab=overview), and [LLM developers](https://app.cbinsights.com/market-report/1710?tab=overview) are also among the top focus areas.

[![](https://research-assets.cbinsights.com/2026/01/08122354/SoV2025_LeadInvestors_7-992x1024.png)](https://research-assets.cbinsights.com/2026/01/08122354/SoV2025_LeadInvestors_7.png)

![](https://research-assets.cbinsights.com/2026/01/08122354/SoV2025_LeadInvestors_7-992x1024.png)

The investments represent a narrowing focus. Traditional venture strategy emphasizes spreading investments across different sectors to reduce risk. Now, the most influential investors are essentially running concentrated AI funds.
